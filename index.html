What I'm working on

herbDigital

Founder

Ideally, technology's evolution has made access to information easy and affordable through professionals and peers' connections. Ideally, this should make information on herbal making and use readily available on the web from different professionals and users. However, this information is scattered worldwide: Youtube, TikTok, Instagram, and other social media platforms. Google searches are tedious when it comes to finding BIPOC related content, for example, scientific achievements of BIPOC. The consequence of this is unorganized data, which is poachable, and this is how we lose the BIPOC legacy of discovery. The dilemma does not just end with locating the information; it extends to the trust we have in these external sources. Do we trust a recipe because it has 2 billion views on Youtube? Most of the time, vloggers tell exciting stories to boost views, so one must embark on a treasure hunt in the comment section, looking for testimonies to the proposed methods. This project provides at-home scientists an opportunity to share their knowledge and be acknowledged for it, which promotes an inclusive and open society. Through this project, we fight racism by having a WOC create a platform that will enable the BIPOC community to have its narrative of STEM documented for future generations. We fight oppressive societal norms in systems that have made it difficult to explore the world of STEM because of lack of opportunity and finances. [link to project]
